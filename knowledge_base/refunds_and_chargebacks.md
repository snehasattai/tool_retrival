# Refunds, Chargebacks, and Disputes Policy

## Refunds
A completed payment can be refunded fully or partially. Refunds cannot
exceed the original payment amount. Refunding a payment is treated as a
sensitive, money-moving action and always requires explicit user
confirmation before it executes.

## What is a dispute?
A dispute is opened by a buyer (e.g. "item not received", "unauthorized
transaction", "significantly not as described"). It starts in the **open**
status. Sellers can respond with a message and evidence; PayPal support may
then move it to **under_review**.

## Dispute lifecycle
1. **open** – buyer has filed a claim.
2. **under_review** – seller has responded, PayPal or the buyer is reviewing.
3. **escalated_claim** – escalated to a formal PayPal claim (binding decision).
4. **resolved** – closed, with a resolution (e.g. accepted_refunded, or a
   custom resolution note).
5. **appealed** – a resolved dispute's decision has been appealed.

## Chargebacks
A chargeback is a dispute initiated through the buyer's bank/card issuer
rather than directly through PayPal. Chargebacks typically carry a higher
fee and a stricter evidence deadline than standard disputes.

## Evidence requirements
Typical evidence PayPal expects to respond to an "item not received" or
"significantly not as described" dispute: proof of shipment, proof of
delivery, and a copy of the original invoice.
